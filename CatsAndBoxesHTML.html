Cats and Boxes - A match made in Heaven



About

Its general cat owner knowledge that cats love boxes and that to cats, no toy in the world, no matter how expensive or fancy it may be, could possibly compare to a simple cardboard box. It's a crazy phenomenon and is not contained to only small cats but any cat big or small participate in this Phenomenon.

Reasoning

The first reason is that cats see boxes as how people would see mediaeval fortresses. Due to their small size when they are in boxes they're surrounded by walls that they can peer over. The things they would hunt are all about bird and mouse size, and the sides of a box for them would be like the walls of a castle. Which is what makes boxes perfect confined spots for them to hunt inside. With cats being ambush predators it means that confined places where they can hide, hunt prey, feel safe and feel warm is an instinctive behaviour so the snug cardboard boxes fit the bill perfectly.

The second reason is curiosity. Cats like to investigate new stuff and also like to get into things to see what's in there. The quote ‘if it fits, it sits’ was created for a reason. Here are some cats in many other different small spaces!

The third reason is that cardboard has an amazing texture. Cardboard is the perfect texture for a cat to bite and scratch, making them great fun to play with. Many owners find that cats love boxes purely to bite and chew and scratch. Fun fact Cats scratch things to express emotions, like excitement or stress, to mark objects with their scent and cardboard is especially good at absorbing the scent.
